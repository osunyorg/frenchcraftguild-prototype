Memoriae (Jewelry Box)
Memoriae  is a jewelry box made of multiple elements and materials. A cup welcomes a touring and openwork porcelain collar. The lid consists of two levels: the first, openwork, is surmounted by an ebony jewel button, set with brass and protected by a porcelain cage. It reveals a second box lined with cork and leather that can contain sixteen rings. A central handle gives access to the heart of the box: a cut in faience patinated by the flames, lined with leather.
Custom-made storey jewelry box in openwork porcelain, sandstone, leather, brass and ebony

Juliette Vivien is a French ceramicist specializing in openwork porcelain, creating hand-crafted craft pieces in her workshop in the Paris region. Initiated very young in ceramics, she trained at the École Duperré and founded her business in 2018. His subtle and contemporary work was rewarded with the Ateliers d’Art de France's Prize of Young Creation in 2020.
