body: >-

Chalice and paten of the Chapter of Notre-Dame de Paris
Chalice and paten commissioned by the Chapter of Notre-Dame de Paris from young designer Julien Roos for the reopening of the cathedral. The two liturgical objects were carved from brass using CNC machining at the MMB company in Normandy, before being electroplated with palladium and subtly satin-finished by microblasting at Bertin Aubert in Paris. Inspired by Gothic architecture, the crown of thorns, and historical sacred objects, this new Eucharistic silverware seems to be animated by a vital force and is the result of a completely new design, both in terms of its design, manufacturing technique, and finish.  
CHALICE:
Height: 31 cm
Cup diameter: 10 cm
Base diameter: 15 cm
Material: palladium-plated brass
Weight: 1540 g (Replica displayed in 3D PLA printing, filler, paint, and varnish)
PATEN:
Height: 3 cm
Diameter: 23.2 cm
Material: palladium-plated brass
Weight: 930 g (Replica displayed in 3D PLA printing, filler, paint, and varnish)

For 30 years, MMB VOLUM-e has been the main supplier of solutions for the additive manufacture of complex parts, polymer and all metal alloys used in industry.

Thanks to an experienced team of various projects, technical explorations and monitoring of technological developments, combined with a unique machine fleet, MMB VOLUM-e carries out the mastery of work, from development to production series, in order to deliver "key-in-hand" projects to its customers.

The pooling of its expertise and resources guarantees its customers optimal responsiveness and adaptability, allowing to meet the demands of the most demanding sectors: aeronautical, space, defence, energy, mobility, equipment goods.

MMB VOLUM-e consists of a "3D Creative Parts" branch, an alliance of rare manual know-how and state-of-the-art technologies to design exceptional pieces in the areas of luxury – perfumery, cosmetics, spirits – jewellery and pharmaceuticals. In 50 years of experience, she took part in many collaborations between the Grand Maisons and the Artists.
