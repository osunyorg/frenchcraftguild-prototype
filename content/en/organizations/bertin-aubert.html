Chalice and paten of the Chapter of Notre-Dame de Paris
Chalice and paten commissioned by the Chapter of Notre-Dame de Paris from young designer Julien Roos for the reopening of the cathedral. The two liturgical objects were carved from brass using CNC machining at the MMB company in Normandy, before being electroplated with palladium and subtly satin-finished by microblasting at Bertin Aubert in Paris. Inspired by Gothic architecture, the crown of thorns, and historical sacred objects, this new Eucharistic silverware seems to be animated by a vital force and is the result of a completely new design, both in terms of its design, manufacturing technique, and finish.  
CHALICE:
Height: 31 cm
Cup diameter: 10 cm
Base diameter: 15 cm
Material: palladium-plated brass
Weight: 1540 g (Replica displayed in 3D PLA printing, filler, paint, and varnish)
PATEN:
Height: 3 cm
Diameter: 23.2 cm
Material: palladium-plated brass
Weight: 930 g (Replica displayed in 3D PLA printing, filler, paint, and varnish)

Located in the heart of Paris in the 10th arrondissement, Maison BERTIN-AUBERT has specialized since 1936 in electrolysis treatments and finishes for the luxury sector (jewelry, fashion, leather goods, music) and industry (aeronautics, space, defense, medical). The company works with a variety of materials (brass, aluminum, stainless steel, tungsten, bronze, steel, as well as silver and gold) using a wide range of precious metal treatments (gold plating, silver plating, palladium, ruthenium, rhodium, bronze) as well as base metals (such as copper, nickel, chrome, zinc, etc.). Bertin Aubert Industries combines excellence and responsibility: EPV label, ISO 9001 certification, GIM CSR charter. The company uses only RJC CoC-certified recycled precious metals and closely follows standards via IUTS and CTACsub 2. It incorporates sustainable practices at every stage of its surface treatments.
